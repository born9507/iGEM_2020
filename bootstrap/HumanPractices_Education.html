Education

Our team wished to encourage more students to learn about this issue; therefore, we took this process even further to educate our entire highschool. Even though our initial plan was to conduct a seminar in the auditorium for high schoolers, a brief introduction to our project along with fun and engaging activities for middle schoolers, and a simple hands-on activity for kindergartners, we were heavily restricted with the gathering we had due to the COVID 19 situation, thus, we adjusted our plans.

From August xxth to the xxth, we presented the post harvest issue and our project only to SISâ€™s high school community in a simple manner. We created an educational and interesting video to inform students in the middle and high school about the importance of addressing a feasible, cost-friendly solution to the immense post-harvest losses that countries face around the world. (add more after the campaign is done) We also had two posters containing different information. The first one, which included data and images, had all the details regarding the local situation in Srilanka and how imperative this issue is.The second poster had a brief design of our sensor and how it will function.

Students got the opportunity to learn about professional concepts such as the E.coli producing the CYP1A2 enzymes along with professional terms like enzymes and genes. Questions were answered regarding both the project and iGEM in general.

Even though some high schoolers already knew about the concept of post harvest, many students showed shallow knowledge or were very indifferent to taking such actions. Thankfully, many students showed big interest in our topic and were wanting to learn more about the concept of post harvest.
