Rice is a staple crop for many countries in Asia.

Rice needs to be dried and kept under specific temperatures after harvest. If not, it loses its merchantability and total yield. In Sri Lanka, the lack of proper management in the post-harvest storage process of rice is causing loss. Most of the fungi that occur in the post-harvest management of rice are Aspergillus spp. 4 species. In this study, we are trying to establish a stringent post-harvest management system for rice by measuring the concentration of aflatoxin B1 (AFB1), a toxin produced by Aspergillus flavus, and Aspergillus parasiticus.

To measure the concentration of the toxin,

we’re going to use cytochrome P450 1A2(CYP1A2). Reaction between aflatoxin B1 and CYP1A2 results in compounds with epoxide residue, which can be used for detection. By constructing a recombinant Escherichia coli that produces CYP1A2, an enzyme required for the sensor, we tried to secure the economics of the management system.
